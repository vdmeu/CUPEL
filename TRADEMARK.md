# Cupel Trademark Policy

The **Cupel** name and logo are trademarks of Eugene Andrie Merwe-Chartier.

This document establishes the trademark policy for the Cupel Professional Competence Provenance Framework.

---

## What Is Open and What Is Protected

The Cupel **specification, schema, and reference implementation** are open source, licensed under AGPL-3.0. Anyone may read, implement, fork, and build upon them without restriction.

The Cupel **name and logo** are trademarked. They signal that an implementation meets the framework's conformance criteria. This protects users from confusion and ensures the Cupel brand remains a reliable trust signal.

This model is used by established open standards including Linux, OpenID, and C2PA.

---

## Permitted Uses (No Permission Required)

You may use the Cupel name freely to:

- Refer to the framework in articles, talks, documentation, or research
- State that your software is "compatible with Cupel" or "implements the Cupel specification"
- Discuss or critique the framework

---

## Uses That Require Permission

You must contact us before using the Cupel name or logo to:

- Name a product, service, or company (e.g. "Cupel Pro", "Cupel Verify")
- Claim your implementation is an official or certified Cupel implementation
- Use the Cupel logo in any form

To request permission: open a GitHub Discussion or email [contact TBD].

---

## Conformance

A conformance programme is under development. Once published, it will define the criteria an implementation must meet to be permitted to use the Cupel name and logo without further approval.
